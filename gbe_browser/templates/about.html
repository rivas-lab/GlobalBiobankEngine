Contributions

    The Global Biobank Engine was built using a modified version of the ExAC browser code-base (developed by the ExAC web development team), and an extended version of the IBD Exomes Browser developed by Manuel Rivas. The Engine development was led by Manuel Rivas. Special thanks to Chris Chang, developer of PLINK2, without whom it would not be possible to complete the work.
    Manuel Rivas and Greg McInnes developed the coding (GWAS) page.
    Greg McInnes and Adam Lavertu connected the submission page for MRP inference engine developed by Manuel A. Rivas and Matti Pirinen.
    Greg McInnes and Yosuke Tanigawa developed Google account authentication.
    Chris DeBoever developed the GBE Power App and the Genetic Correlation App. Boths apps were conceived and designed by Manuel A. Rivas.
    Yosuke Tanigawa developed variant (PheWAS) page and the Decomposition App was conceived and designed by Manuel A. Rivas.
    Matthew Aguirre developed the risk modeling page. The risk modeling page was conceived and designed by Manuel A. Rivas.
    Julia Eve Olivieri developed the HLA Disease Map App. The HLA Disease Map App was conceived and designed by Manuel A. Rivas.

Terms of use

To cite the browser, please use: Global Biobank Engine, Stanford, CA (URL: http://gbe.stanford.edu) [date (month, year) accessed].

You can freely browse the association results.

Citation in publications

We request that any use of data obtained from the Global Biobank Engine be cited in publications using the following format:

  • Global Biobank Engine, Stanford, CA (URL: http://gbe.stanford.edu) [date (month, year) accessed].
  • G. McInnes, Y. Tanigawa, C. DeBoever, A. Lavertu, J. E. Olivieri, M. Aguirre, M. A. Rivas, Global Biobank Engine: enabling genotype-phenotype browsing for biobank summary statistics. Bioinformatics (2019), doi:10.1093/bioinformatics/bty999.
